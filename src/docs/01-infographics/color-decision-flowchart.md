## Common Confusion Points

### ü§î BLUE vs VIOLET ‚Äî When to use which?

**Use BLUE when:**
- Talking about EXISTING reliability/trust
- Security features that PROTECT
- Safety protocols that PREVENT harm
- Information that INFORMS users
- Systems that are STABLE now

**Example:** "Your data is encrypted" (passive protection)

**Use VIOLET when:**
- Talking about IMPROVING over time
- AI systems that LEARN
- Memory that RETAINS knowledge
- Features that GET BETTER
- Adaptive systems that EVOLVE

**Example:** "AI learns your preferences" (active improvement)

---

### ü§î GOLD vs VIOLET ‚Äî Financial analytics or AI analytics?

**Use GOLD when:**
- Focus is on MONEY/VALUE
- ROI and financial returns
- Premium pricing and luxury
- Business performance metrics

**Example:** "Increased revenue by $2M"

**Use VIOLET when:**
- Focus is on INTELLIGENCE/PREDICTION
- AI-powered insights
- Predictive analytics
- Learning algorithms

**Example:** "AI predicts 94% accuracy"

---

### ü§î TEAL vs VIOLET ‚Äî Tech innovation?

**Use TEAL when:**
- Hardware/physical technology
- Wearables and devices
- Operational systems
- Infrastructure and processes

**Example:** "Smart glasses product line"

**Use VIOLET when:**
- Software intelligence
- AI and machine learning
- Adaptive algorithms
- Knowledge systems

**Example:** "AI assistant learns your workflow"

## Section Background Alternation

### For Multi-Section Pages:

```
PATTERN:
Dark section ‚Üí Light section ‚Üí Dark section ‚Üí Light section

ACCENT STRATEGY:
Hero (Dark): Sage/Emerald
¬ß1 (Light): Warm accent (Gold, Coral) or Violet
¬ß2 (Dark): Cool accent (Violet, Teal) or Blue
¬ß3 (Light): Warm accent (Gold) or Blue
¬ß4 (Dark): Cool accent (Violet, Blue, Teal)
¬ß5 (Light): Any accent based on content
Closing (Light): Emerald (brand)
```

### Balance Principles:

1. **Alternate light/dark** for visual rhythm
2. **Warm accents on light** backgrounds (gold, coral, violet)
3. **Cool accents on dark** backgrounds (blue, violet, teal)
4. **Max 2 accents per section** (avoid rainbow effect)
5. **Group related sections** with same color family

## Accessibility Quick Check

### Minimum Contrast Ratios (WCAG AA):

**On Light Backgrounds:**
- ‚úÖ Emerald: 7.8:1 (AAA)
- ‚úÖ Gold: 4.9:1 (AA)
- ‚úÖ Blue: 4.2:1 (AA for large text)
- ‚úÖ Coral: 4.5:1 (AA)
- ‚úÖ Violet: 5.1:1 (AA)
- ‚úÖ Teal: 6.2:1 (AA)

**On Dark Backgrounds:**
- ‚úÖ All colors: 5:1+ minimum with #FAF9F7 text

**Rule:** Always use cream (#FAF9F7) or light sage (#D4E6D0) text on dark backgrounds with accent colors.

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

‚ùå **Don't use blue for AI learning**
- Wrong: "AI learns your habits" + Blue
- Right: "AI learns your habits" + Violet

‚ùå **Don't use violet for static security**
- Wrong: "Encrypted storage" + Violet
- Right: "Encrypted storage" + Blue

‚ùå **Don't use gold for non-financial analytics**
- Wrong: "User engagement analytics" + Gold
- Right: "User engagement analytics" + Emerald or Violet

‚ùå **Don't mix warm and cool accents in same section**
- Wrong: Gold card + Blue card + Teal card
- Right: Gold card + Coral card (both warm)

‚ùå **Don't use more than 2 accent colors per section**
- Creates visual chaos and dilutes meaning

## Testing Checklist

Before finalizing color choice, ask:

- [ ] Does this color's semantic meaning match the content?
- [ ] Is contrast ratio minimum 4.5:1 for text?
- [ ] Am I alternating light/dark section backgrounds?
- [ ] Am I using warm accents on light, cool on dark (mostly)?
- [ ] Have I limited to max 2 accent colors in this section?
- [ ] Would a colorblind user still understand the hierarchy?
- [ ] Does this fit the calm, sophisticated brand tone?
